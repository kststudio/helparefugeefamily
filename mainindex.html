Summary

When you hear about the refugee crisis, you often won’t hear their names. Most refuse to show their faces for fear of retribution. One thing is certain. They all have a story and they all want to be saved. The only problem is , the only government willing to save them in masses and offer them a future is Germany. Which lies nearly 2000 miles north of the middle east.

The Rouk family banded together to make the daunting decision of leaving everything they knew and worked for their entire lives in Syria. They leave because war has torn their lives apart, taken people they loved, starved them, and robbed them of every opportunity of having a promising future.

With that, they decided escaping the throlls of war would be worth the uncertainties of traveling across Europe by any means possible with over a dozen children in tow. It would have a greater future reward than waiting, surrounded by death, for the war to end.

After a grueling journey, they now await asylum in Germany. Unfortunately, it’s not over for them. Resources are first come first serve. With their variety of needs, someone always goes without.

We are here today, to help them ease into their new lives, offer them the best tools to integrate and to allow these children, to be children.

The Journey

  • September 10

    They crossed Turkish waters to the Greek Island, Chios on a 25 person dinghy with nearly 50 people.

  • September 15

    After sleeping outside for 4 nights, they boarded a ferry that took them to mainland Greece immediately made their way north.

  • September 16

    MA bus dropped them off in Serbia, where they would walk for nearly 6 hours across the Croatian border into the rural town, Tovarnik.

  • September 19

    They boarded a cargo train from Tovarnik and stood for nearly 7 hours in the middle of the night until they reached the Hungarian border near Nickelsdorf, Austria.

  • September 20

    A bus took them to Klangenfurt, Austria where they would sleep indoors for the first time since beginning their journey.
